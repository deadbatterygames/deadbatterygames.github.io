System Fault

Play System Fault

Objective:

The objective of System Fault is to gather Energy and find blueprints for various ship modules which help you progress through the solar system. Currently, the game spawns you in a test scene with all the blueprints and modules available immediately to the player.

Equipment:

There are two pieces of equipment available to the player: the Matter Manipulator, and the Multicannon. The Matter Manipulator allows the player to dematerialize ship modules and connect them to the ship, or equip a Fuel Pack on the player's back. The Fuel Pack allows the player to collect Energy, and also gives the player shields to protect them from damage. The Multicannon is the weapon available to the player, which will eventually have multiple types the player can switch between, allowing for a rock-paper-scissors combat system.

Collecting Energy:

In order to collect Energy, the player must have a Fuel Pack equipped on their back. To do so, switch to the Matter Manipulator using TAB, pick up a Fuel Pack (the rectangular ship module) using LMB, and click RMB to equip the Fuel Pack. Note the health bar that appears at the top - this is the best way to determine whether or not you currently have a Fuel Pack equipped. To remove the pack, press RMB with the Matter Manipulator equipped. Make sure you aren't holding another object, or this will not work. Once a Fuel Pack is equipped on the player's back, use the Multicannon to shoot Energy crystals, and walk over the shards to collect them.

Blueprints:

Each of the orange terminals will unlock a specific blueprint that the player can use at Part Printers. Eventually these terminals will be rewarded to the player at the end of Refineries, which are underground dungeons built by the enemy robots. If the player hasn't collected any blueprints, the Tier 1 Fuel Pack will be available for free.

Part Printers:

The blue terminals on the landing pads allow the player to print parts from a list of blueprints they have collected. Press E to interact with the terminal, A/D to change the module, W/S to change the module's tier, and SPACE to print the module. In order to print a part, the player must have a Fuel Pack equipped (the rectangular ship module) with enough Energy, and the print surface must be relatively clear. Press E again to exit the terminal.

Modular Space Ship:

Each module of the ship has a separate functionality: Fuel Packs power the ship and the its shields, Boosters allow you to turn and strafe, Thrusters allow you to fly forward and back and Quantum Drives allow you to jump to other planets. Each module has 3 tiers which are indicated by the bronze/silver/gold colour of the module. To take off, make sure at least a Fuel Pack and Boosters (the module that goes into the wings) are connected to your ship, and the Fuel Pack has fuel, then press SPACE to power on your ship. Note: You cannot enter your ship while wearing a Fuel Pack. Click RMB with the Matter Manipulator equipped to remove your Fuel Pack and LMB to attach it to your ship's appropriate slot.

Space Ship Assist Modes:

The space ship has 3 assist modes available: Hover Mode (H), which allows you to make fine adjustments to the ship at a low speed, Astro Flight (A) which allows you to adjust a throttle controlling your ship's constant speed, and Quantum Jump (Q) which allows you to jump to other planets. Press SHIFT to toggle between Hover Mode and Astro Flight, and press Z while looking at a planet to Quantum Jump. A planet can be targeted in free look (press RMB to toggle), in which case a reticle will be displayed showing the direction the ship needs to turn before the jump is initiated.

Controls:

WASD: Move player / ship
SPACE: Jump / Power space ship (when inside) / Print ship module
E: Interact / Exit ship (when powered off)
C: Charge shields with Fuel (must be wearing a Fuel Pack)
LMB: Fire Multicannon / Pick up module with Matter Manipulator
RMB: Equip Fuel Pack with Matter Manipulator / Toggle free look (while in ship)
TAB: Change equipment
R/F: Ship vertical
Q/E: Ship roll
LEFT SHIFT: Cycle ship assist modes (Hover Mode, Astro Flight)
LEFT CTRL: Toggle ship assist
Z: Quantum Jump to targeted planet (can be targeted using free look, or by maneuvering ship) V: Toggle camera view (while in ship)
